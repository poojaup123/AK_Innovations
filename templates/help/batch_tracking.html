Batch Tracking Guide

Complete traceability from raw materials to finished products

What is Batch Tracking?

Batch tracking provides complete traceability of materials throughout the manufacturing process. Each batch has a unique identifier that follows the material from receipt through production to final delivery.

Batch Lifecycle

Receipt

Batch created when materials arrive

Storage

Batch stored in designated location

Production

Batch consumed in manufacturing

Transfer

Batch moved between processes

Completion

Finished product batches created

Dispatch

Batch shipped to customer

Batch Information

Basic Information:
  • Batch Number (unique identifier)
  • Item Code and Description
  • Quantity and Unit of Measure
  • Manufacturing Date
  • Expiry Date (if applicable)
Process Information:
  • Current Process/Location
  • Quality Status
  • Supplier Information
  • Production Order Reference
  • Processing History

Multi-State Tracking

Batches are tracked through different manufacturing states:

Raw Material

Materials as received from suppliers

Work in Progress

Materials being processed

Finished Goods

Completed products ready for sale

Scrap

Rejected or unusable material

Traceability Features

Forward Traceability: Track where specific batches went and which products they became part of
Backward Traceability: Identify which raw material batches were used in a finished product
Supplier Traceability: Link finished products back to original suppliers
Process Traceability: Track which processes and operators worked on each batch

Quality Management

Batch tracking supports comprehensive quality control:

  • Incoming Inspection: Record quality status upon receipt
  • Process Quality: Track quality at each manufacturing stage
  • Final Inspection: Ensure products meet specifications
  • Customer Complaints: Quickly identify affected batches
  • Recall Management: Precise identification of products to recall

Expiry Management

For time-sensitive materials:

FIFO (First In, First Out): Automatic suggestion to use older batches first
Expiry Alerts: Notifications for approaching expiry dates
Shelf Life Tracking: Calculate remaining useful life

Batch Reports

Available reports include:

  • Batch Movement Report: Complete history of batch transactions
  • Batch Genealogy: Family tree showing parent-child relationships
  • Where Used Report: Shows where specific batches were consumed
  • Lot Traceability: End-to-end tracking from supplier to customer
  • Expiry Report: Batches approaching expiry
Critical for Compliance: Batch tracking is essential for ISO certification, customer audits, regulatory compliance, and effective recall management.
