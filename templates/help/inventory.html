Inventory Management Guide

Complete guide to managing your factory inventory

Overview

The Inventory Management system tracks all materials, products, and components throughout your manufacturing process. It supports multi-state inventory tracking, batch management, and real-time stock monitoring.

Multi-State Inventory

Items are tracked in different states throughout the manufacturing process:

Raw Material

Items as received from suppliers, ready for production

Work in Progress

Items currently being processed or in job work

Finished Goods

Completed items ready for sale or shipment

Scrap

Rejected or unusable material

Adding New Items

Item Code: Unique identifier for the item. Use a consistent naming convention (e.g., MTL-001, PRD-001)
HSN Code: Required for GST compliance. Use proper HSN codes for accurate tax calculation
Unit of Measure: Choose the most appropriate unit for tracking this item (Pcs, Kg, M, etc.)
Minimum Stock: Set alert level to prevent stockouts

Stock Movements

Stock movements are automatically tracked through:

  • Purchase Orders: Increases raw material stock when received
  • Production: Converts raw materials to finished goods
  • Job Work: Moves items between different WIP states
  • Sales Orders: Reduces finished goods stock when shipped
  • Manual Adjustments: For corrections and physical count updates

Batch Tracking

Batch tracking provides complete traceability:

Benefits:
  • Quality control and recall management
  • Expiry date tracking
  • Manufacturing process history
  • Supplier traceability

Inventory Reports

Available reports include:

  • Stock Summary: Current stock levels by item
  • Low Stock Alert: Items below minimum stock level
  • Stock Movement: Transaction history for any period
  • Batch Report: Batch-wise stock analysis
  • Valuation Report: Inventory value calculation
Important: Always verify stock movements are accurately recorded. Incorrect inventory data affects production planning, costing, and financial reporting.
