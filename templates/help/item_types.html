Item Types Classification Guide

Understanding when and how to classify your inventory items

What are Item Types?

Item Types are fundamental classifications that determine how your system handles each item throughout your manufacturing process. They affect inventory tracking, costing, accounting, and reporting.

Quick Decision Tree

Ask yourself:
  1. What happens to this item? Does it become part of a product, get consumed, or stay as a tool?
  2. How is it used? In production, for maintenance, for packaging, or sold as-is?
  3. How should it be accounted for? As inventory, expense, or asset?
Material

Use for: Raw materials, components, and inputs that get transformed

Examples: Steel sheets, bolts, screws, aluminum rods, plastic pellets, chemicals, wire, rubber, glass

Inventory Behavior: Purchased → consumed in production → becomes part of finished goods

Accounting: Raw material expense when consumed in production

Product

Use for: Finished goods you manufacture and sell

Examples: Completed assemblies, final products, manufactured items ready for sale, finished components

Inventory Behavior: Produced from materials → stored as finished goods → sold to customers

Accounting: Cost of goods sold when sold, inventory asset when in stock

Consumable

Use for: Items consumed during production but don't become part of the final product

Examples: Cutting oil, welding gas, cleaning solvents, sandpaper, lubricants, flux, electrodes

Inventory Behavior: Purchased → consumed during manufacturing processes

Accounting: Manufacturing overhead expense when used

Tool

Use for: Reusable equipment and tools for production

Examples: Cutting tools, drill bits, measuring instruments, jigs, fixtures, dies, molds

Inventory Behavior: Purchased → used repeatedly → eventually replaced due to wear

Accounting: Fixed asset (depreciated) or expense depending on value

Spare Part

Use for: Replacement components for machinery and equipment

Examples: Motor bearings, pump seals, conveyor belts, electrical components, filters, gaskets

Inventory Behavior: Purchased → stored → used for maintenance when needed

Accounting: Maintenance expense when consumed

Packaging

Use for: Materials used to package and ship finished products

Examples: Boxes, bubble wrap, labels, pallets, shrink wrap, tape, foam inserts

Inventory Behavior: Purchased → used to package finished goods for shipment

Accounting: Packaging expense or part of cost of goods sold

Common Classification Scenarios

Material vs Consumable: If it becomes part of the final product = Material. If it's used up during production = Consumable
Tool vs Spare Part: If it's used in production = Tool. If it's for machinery maintenance = Spare Part
Product vs Material: If you sell it directly = Product. If it goes into making something else = Material

Important Guidelines

Consistency is key: Use the same classification for similar items across your operations
For multi-use items: Choose the primary use case in your factory
When changing types: This affects historical reporting and accounting - consider carefully before changing existing items

Impact on Your System

Your Item Type selection drives:

  • Inventory Tracking: How stock movements are recorded
  • Costing: How costs are calculated and allocated
  • Accounting: Which accounts are used for transactions
  • Reporting: How items appear in various reports
  • BOM Management: How items can be used in Bill of Materials
  • Production Planning: How items are scheduled and tracked
